Books

Here is a collection of my thoughts on the books I am reading this year.

Every Tool's a Hammer - Adam Savage

An entertaining read that I felt a connection to as Mythbusters was a part of my childhood/adolescence.

One of the main takeaways I got from reading it was that everyone has their thing; everyone is creative in one way or another. That can take the form of sewing, cooking, programming, singing, writing, model making, cosplay and so on. Everyone has interests they are "into" and that is something to be celebrated and embraced and shared. It takes vulnerability, but doing so you will find there are others who share your passion. You will also find there are others who don't. But the more you share, the more you will find.

Sharing was another big theme of the book. Share your visions, hopes, dreams, failures, ideas, and knowledge. I loved the quote: "Knowledge is power, and the most powerful thing you can do with knowledge is give it away." I am a supporter of sharing, even though I sometimes struggle with it.

It was entertaining to step into Adam's mind and see his growth. From an impatient naieve youngster to an impatient but seasoned adult, he calls out some of his growth and lessons learned.

All the ideas and anecdotes he shares is his attempt to share some of the things he has learned to, hopefully, help and encourage others to lean into their interests.

Everyone has their thing, and everyone can be creative. Lean into it, embrace your weirdness, and be prepared to learn, grow, and share it.
